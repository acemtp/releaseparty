Not a pitch-athon

No rank, no judges, no slides, no final pitch. You are here to create awesome things. Don't expect to launch a startup or go to SF. You don't have a business model? That's the point! There's no market for your hack? Even better!

By DOers for DOers

Set up by DOers for DOers. You are novice or expert, young or old, programmer, artist, musician, hardware hacker, as long as you want to do something you are more than welcome. Tourists are pleased to stay at home.

Release

Release is the key word of the weekend. Release something before the end of the weekend and show it to your peers.

Collaboration & creativity

Release Party is a place to collaborate, share and invent the weirdest, coolest, most creative things. you are strongly invited to help other people!

Web, Mobile, Game, Hardware

You want to create a game, a piece of hardware, a webapp, a desktop app, a mobile service or a hardware-game-on-Internet-readyformobile? This hackathon is for you!

Last minute theme

To make things funnier, all the projects will have to follow a theme. This theme will be kept secret until the grand opening Friday evening. You'll have only 30mn of intense brainstorm to find a cool project.

Fast compo

Because it's not hard enough to release a project in 40 hours, we'll challenge you with fast compo during the week end, accept it and show us that nothing can scare you. Fast compo are of course rewarded with badges.

Achievements & Badges

You are a collector? Everything during this party is linked to badges. Select which achievements you want to do and go try to get them. Some examples of badges you can get? Opensourcer, Releaser, Hardware, Game, Web, Sponsor, Mentor, FastCompo, ... The full list will be available the D day.

FAQ

Why another damn hackathon?

Yes, there are hackathon every week end. Vianney organized the Paris version of AngelHack, the biggest hackathon. He also won some hackathons like the BeMyApp (http://cleverdoor.co). In the previous century, Vianney was taking part of the DemoScene that regularly organize CodingParties. A Hackathon and a CodingParty is almost the same thing, pack some motivated people in the same room for a week end. But the 2 doesn't share the same philosophy. Today most hackathon are place to win money, start a startup and often, the winner is the one who has the best pitch. In contrast, during coding party, you only show what you do, the project speaks for you and more important, you go to a coding party to meet amazing people and have a memorable experience, not only to win.

The Release Party takes the best of the Hackathon, the Coding Parties but also other similar events like GlobalGameJam (see it like hackathon for game) to do a unique event.

Why should I come?

  • to have an memorable experience
  • to show to your peers what amazing things you can do in only a week end
  • to meet and talk with awesome people
  • to leave your comfort zone and learn lot of new things

Why it's not free?

Tickets fee is really small. It doesn't even cover the food expenses. We just want people who join the Release Party be commited and really want to be part of the experience.

Where & when?

It'll take place in Paris in November. More info soon.

How to get a ticket?

For the moment, tickets are not for sell. Subscribe on the homepage to be contacted when they are available.

Will there be prizes?

Sponsors will be able to attach some prizes to achievements. Depending of which achievement you plan to get, you'll decide which prize you can win.
